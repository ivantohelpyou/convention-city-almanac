## Scale Without Consolidation

LA scores 29 — high in absolute terms but lower than NYC (38) despite being the larger metro. The difference: NYC's participation infrastructure is dense and transit-accessible (three library systems, CUNY, subway). LA's is spread across 500 square miles, car-dependent, and harder to access without a vehicle.

**The 99-seat theater tradition** is LA's analog to Chicago's storefront theaters — hundreds of small companies operating under the Equity waiver system, where actors can perform without union contracts in venues under 99 seats. This is genuine participation theater at scale.

**The Brewery** — the world's largest art colony (600+ artists in a former Pabst brewery) — is a participation asset no other city matches on arts production scale.
